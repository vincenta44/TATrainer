Notes on Requirement Gathering

Requirement Gathering 101

- 10 rules for successful requirement gathering

  1. Don't assume you know what the customer wants- alway
  2. Involve the users from the start
  3. Define and agree one the scope of the project
  4. Make sure requirments are SMART
    • specific
    • measurable
    • agreed upon
    • realistic
    • time-based
  5. Gain clarity if there is any doubt
  6. Create a ckear, concise and thorough requirements document and share it with the customer
  7. Confirm your understanding of the requirements alongside the customer
  8. Avoid talking technology or solutions until the requirements are fully understood
  9. Get the requirements agreed with the stakeholders before the project starts
  10. Create a prototype, if necessary, to confirm or refine the customer's requirements
- Example of Common Mistakes
  • Basing a solution on complex or cutting-edge technology and then discovering that it cannot easily be rolled out in the 'real world'
  • Insufficient consultation with real users and practitioners
  • Lacking a clear understanding and making assumptions rather than asking

10 Techniques for Gathering Requirements

  1. One-one-one interviews
  2. Group Interviews
  3. Facilitated Sessions
  4. Joint Applicaiton Development
  5. Questionnaires
  6. Prototyping
  7. Use Cases
  8. Following People Around
  9. Request for Proposals
  10. Brainstorming

Things to keep in mind

  • Identify and involve a representative set of stakeholders
  • Seek breadth before depth
  • Iterate and clarify
  • Prioritize
  • Use the stakeholder's terminology
  • Employ KISS (keep it simple but be thorough)
  • Realize that you will never get a complete set of requirements up-front; some won't surface until the stakeholders have pieces of the solution that they can see and touch
  • Remember goals and objectives are not requirements
  • Requirements and constraints should drive the solution not the other way around
